Background And Fornation Of National Association Of Ngerian Students'(NANS)

Background:
The National Association Of Nigerian Students'(NANS) is the apex student organization in Nigeria,representing the interests of Nigerian Students both domestically and internationally.

  • Founded:1980

  • Predecessor:National Union Of Nigerian Students(NUNS)

  • Reason For Formation:

    After the military government banned NUNS in 1078 following the "Ali Must Go Protest"(Against increased tuition fees and then Education Minister,Col.Ahmadu Ali),students regrouped the form NANS in 1980

Key Events Leading To NANS' Birth

  • 1978 "Ali Must Go Protest: Nationwide students protests against fee hikes led to deaths and injuries. NUNS was proscribed,and several student leaders were explelled.

  • 1980:At a meeting in Bayero University,Kano,students from various institutions founded NANS to continue the legacy of NUNS and advocate for student rights.

Objecives Of NANS

  1. Defend the rights and welfare of Nigerian students.


  2. Promote educatonal reforms and resist policies that harm students.


  3. Serve as a voice against dictatorship,corruption and oppression.


  4. Unite students across tertiary institutions under one national platform.


STRUCTURE AND OPERATIONS

  1. National Headquarters: Often rotates depending on the elected president.


  2. Zones: Nigerian is divided into 6-8 zones (A-F,Sometimes G & H) for regional coordination.


  3. Annual Convention: Elective congress where new national officers are chosen


  4. Affiliations: Some NANS factions also participate in international student movements
